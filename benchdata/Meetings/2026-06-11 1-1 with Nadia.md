# 1-1 with Nadia

## Campaign Queue Health
We reviewed the current CowQM setup for campaign processing and agreed that queue lag is now the clearest signal of user-facing risk. When the campaign queue backs up, background jobs still complete, but operators lose confidence because completion times become unpredictable. We want a simple dashboard that shows waiting, active, failed, and delayed counts together with age of oldest job.

## Observability and Alerts
Nadia suggested treating CowQM metrics as a first-class operational surface rather than a debugging aid. The dashboard should highlight queue saturation, retry spikes, and worker throughput by campaign type. We also want alerts that fire on sustained lag, repeated job failures, and dead-letter queue growth, so the team gets warned before support tickets appear.

## Replay and Failure Handling
We discussed a safer replay flow for failed background jobs. Instead of manually re-enqueueing items one by one, we want a controlled replay path from the dead-letter queue with reason codes preserved. That would let us rerun only the campaign jobs affected by a transient dependency issue while avoiding duplicate work or accidental broad retries.

## Next Steps
Prototype the operational dashboard, define alert thresholds, and document when a job should move from normal retries into the dead-letter queue.
