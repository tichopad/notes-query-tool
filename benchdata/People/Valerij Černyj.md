# Valerij Černyj

## Role
A synthetic knowledge systems librarian who maintains internal note collections for a small research workshop. He organizes messy project material, writes concise summaries, and builds lightweight tagging rules so people can rediscover decisions later. In meetings he usually says little at first, then asks one precise question that clarifies the whole discussion.

## Projects
His current focus is a benchmark set for searching personal notes, especially names with diacritics and near matches. He keeps a test notebook of ambiguous contacts, transliterated terms, and repeated false positives to compare retrieval behavior across keyword, fuzzy, and semantic search modes. Another side project is a compact glossary of Czech and English terms used in archive descriptions.

## Habits
Valerij prefers quiet morning work, black tea without sugar, and plain text tools over heavy apps. He writes short daily logs, marks unresolved items with a single question mark, and reviews search failures every Friday. When a note feels too vague, he adds one concrete detail rather than more categories.

## Context
Colleagues know him as patient, exact, and mildly skeptical of automation claims. He likes clean folder structures, durable filenames, and notes that remain readable years later.
